What you will learn in this unit

This module covers the essentials of map tasks. You will learn to describe changes over time as shown on a map, use prepositions to explain location, and use past tense verb forms (active and passive) to describe change — the essential skills every map answer must demonstrate without errors.

The 4-paragraph plan

¶ContentExample
1 Introductionparaphrase: what, where, whenThe maps show the changes which took place in Youngsville between 1990 and 2010.
2 Overviewthe biggest general trend, no detailsOverall, the town changed from a largely rural to a mainly urban landscape.
3 Body Aone half of the map (e.g. south of the river)South of the river, all the trees were cut down…

🎯 Examiner insight: there are three important parts in every answer — an introductory sentence, an overview, and body paragraphs describing what the maps show. A missing overview severely limits your Task Achievement score.
📌 Method: ① number the changes on the second map → ② divide the town into two logical halves (north/south, east/west, or by a river/railway) → ③ describe each half in order (e.g. west → east) → ④ note what did NOT change.

Grammar point 1 · Prepositions & the language of location

The golden IN vs TO rule

PatternMeaningExample
in the north of…INSIDE the areaThe airport is in the south-west of the town. Phu My Hung is in the south of HCM city.
to the north of… / north of…OUTSIDE the area, or relative to another featureChina is to the north of Vietnam. Only a few trees (to the) north of the river remained.

Prepositions of place

be located/situated in · opposite / on the opposite side of · across from · next to / beside · between … and … · along · on the banks of (river) · on the shores of (lake) · by the sea · just off the coastline · in the top right-hand corner · in the middle of · surrounded by · run from … to …

⚠ Don't say "the left/right side of the town" — compass points only (in the north-east of the town). "On the left of the map" is acceptable.

Grammar point 2 · Past simple — regular and irregular

When both maps are in the past (or past → today), the changes are described mainly in the past simple. Regular verbs add -ed (expanded, increased, created, developed, moved, changed); irregular verbs must be memorised (made, built, was/were, became, cut, dug, caught, underwent).

Grammar point 3 · Past simple passive — the map tense

We usually don't know who changed the town, so the passive is the natural voice: was/were + past participle.

CompareThe university developed the campus. (active — subject does the action)
New halls of residence were built. (passive — subject receives the action)
⚠ Never passive: take place, become, change (of a place), make way for, appear, disappear, remain. Say A number of changes took place — never "were taken place".

Grammar point 4 · "By + date" + past perfect

Use the past perfect (had done / had been done) to describe changes completed before a specific time in the past:

TechniqueBy 2012, the block of flats had been turned into a hotel. (passive)
By 2012, the block of flats had made way for a hotel. (active)

This single structure adds real variety to your grammatical range.

6 · Model answers — read like an examiner

introduction overview / summary body detail

Model — Sunnyhills University campus (1995 → today)

The two maps show changes to the campus of Sunnyhills University between 1995 and today. We can see that the university made many changes during this period, including new buildings and recreation facilities.

In the past, the Scott Library was in front of the Founder's Building. It was relocated to the right, and in addition, it increased in size. The old library building now contains laboratories. In 1995 there was a car park in the top left-hand corner of the campus, whereas now the main reception is there. The halls of residence were moved to the far left of the campus and a new Arts Building was built opposite them.

The old sports ground, gym and tennis courts were demolished; however, in their place the university created a new recreation area with a pond, trees and seating areas. What is more, the university developed new sports facilities, including table tennis tables, a new gym and squash courts. They also built two new cafés.

So, it is clear that the university changed and expanded during this period.

Task Achievement · Find and organise the main features

Task Achievement rewards answers that cover all the main changes, include a clear overview, and leave out irrelevant detail. Before writing: ① circle every change on the second map, ② decide which are MAIN changes (large, obvious, affecting whole zones) and which are minor, ③ sort them into two body groups, ④ write one overview sentence about the general trend.

🎯 Examiner insight: an answer that reports six main changes clearly scores higher than one that lists every tree and lamppost. Selection IS the skill being tested.
