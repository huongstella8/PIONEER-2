Module 1: Mastering Complete Sentences
Every complete sentence needs a Subject and a Verb — missing either creates a fragment that lowers your IELTS band score.
Theory The Golden Rule & Common Errors ▼

⭐ The Golden Rule

Every complete sentence in English must have at least one Subject (who/what) and one Verb (action/state). A sentence missing either is called a fragment.

❌ Error A — Missing Subject

IncorrectIs essential to protect the environment. (What is essential?)
CorrectIt is essential to protect the environment.

❌ Error B — Missing Verb

IncorrectThe percentage of university students very high. (No verb!)
CorrectThe percentage of university students is very high.
💡 Tip: In IELTS essays, students often forget the subject when listing advantages, and forget to be (is/are/am) when describing charts.

Module 2: Mastering Adjectives for Descriptive Accuracy
Use adjectives correctly — placement, order, and form all matter in IELTS writing.
Theory 4 Golden Rules + Common Errors ▼

Rule 1 be + adjective

She is intelligent. · The results are significant.

Rule 2 adjective + noun

He is a serious person. · The chart shows a steep line.

Rule 3 Multiple adjectives follow OSASCOMP order

OpinionSizeAgeShapeColorOriginMaterialPurpose
Example: a beautiful [Op] large [Si] old [Ag] red [Co] French [Or] car

Rule 4 NEVER add -s to adjectives

Those girls are good-looking. NOT: good-lookings

❌ Error A – Missing Verb (Rule 1)

IncorrectLiving in a city very expensive.
CorrectLiving in a city is very expensive.

❌ Error B – Backward Word Order (Rule 2)

IncorrectThe chart shows a line steep.
CorrectThe chart shows a steep line.

❌ Error C – Wrong Order (Rule 3)

IncorrectWe bought a German beautiful old car.
CorrectWe bought a beautiful old German car.

❌ Error D – Plural Adjective (Rule 4)

IncorrectThe country faces severes economic challenges.
CorrectThe country faces severe economic challenges.

Module 3: Simple and Compound Sentences
Connect ideas correctly with FANBOYS conjunctions and master when to use (or skip) the comma.
Theory Simple vs. Compound Sentences ▼

Rule 1 Simple Sentence

One subject-verb combination. Can have multiple subjects OR multiple verbs — but it is still ONE independent clause.

2 Subjects + 1 Verb My friends and I watched the game.
1 Subject + 2 Verbs We watched the game and cheered for our team.

Rule 2 Compound Sentence — FANBOYS

Two independent clauses joined by a comma + coordinating conjunction.
forandnorbutoryetso
Formula: [Independent Clause 1] , [and / but / so…] [Independent Clause 2]

❌ Error A – Fake Compound (Unnecessary Comma)

IncorrectThe government built new roads, and improved public transport.
CorrectThe government built new roads and improved public transport.

❌ Error B – Missing Comma

IncorrectThe city is very crowded so pollution is a major problem.
CorrectThe city is very crowded, so pollution is a major problem.

Module 4: Complex Sentences & Eliminating Fragments
Time clauses (when/before/after) create complex sentences — but they need the right comma placement.
Theory Complex Sentences with Time Clauses ▼

Rule 1 Time Clause First → Use a Comma

when / before / after + [dependent clause], [independent clause]

When we said goodbye, everybody cried.

Rule 2 Time Clause Second → NO Comma

[independent clause] when / before / after [dependent clause]

Everybody cried when we said goodbye.

❌ Error A – Fragment (Dependent Clause Alone)

Incorrect (Fragment)After I checked my bag.
CorrectAfter I checked my bag, I went through airport security.

❌ Error B – Unnecessary Comma

IncorrectI went to Buenos Aires, after I finished school.
CorrectI went to Buenos Aires after I finished school.

Module 5: Listing Order & Adverbs of Probability
Organize your ideas professionally and place adverbs in the right position for IELTS band 7+.
Theory Listing Words & Adverb Placement Rules ▼

Rule 1 Listing Order Words

Use: First of all, Second, Third, In addition, Furthermore, Finally, Most of all,

⚠️ Avoid "Also" at the start of a sentence in IELTS essays — it sounds informal. Put also before the main verb instead:
InformalAlso, the city has great public transport.
Academic (Fix 1)The city also has great public transport.
Academic (Fix 2)Furthermore, the city has great public transport.

Rule 2 Adverbs of Probability

Maybe / Perhaps → before the subject

✓Perhaps she is going to transfer.

Probably / Definitely → after will or be

✓We are definitely not going.
We will probably arrive late.

Module 6: Eliminating Run-On Sentences
Joining two independent clauses without proper punctuation or a conjunction creates a run-on error.
Theory What Is a Run-On? ▼

❌ Error A – No Connecting Word

Run-OnI am interested in police work my major is criminology.
FixedI am interested in police work, and my major is criminology.

❌ Error B – Comma Splice

Run-OnI left him a message, he called me back.
FixedAfter I left him a message, he called me back.
💡 Fix options: Add a FANBOYS conjunction with a comma · Use a subordinating conjunction (after/when/before) · Split into two sentences.

Module 7: Capitalization & Listing with Commas
Know exactly what to capitalize and how to separate items in a series correctly.
Theory Capitalization Rules & Series Commas ▼

✅ DO Capitalize

Specific buildings: The White House
Organizations: Stanford University
Months/days/holidays: January, Monday, Ramadan
Specific regions (as names): the Middle East, Southeast Asia

❌ Do NOT Capitalize

Seasons: spring, summer, fall, winter
Compass directions (when giving direction): drive south, turn west

Commas in a Series (3+ items)

IncorrectJohn Mary and I are classmates.
CorrectJohn, Mary, and I are classmates.
